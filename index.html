The NPI philosophy

NPI isn't a phase.
It's the window.

Every product will eventually move to High-Volume Manufacturing. That's inevitable. The real question — the one most companies never ask clearly enough — is: was it fully developed and improved while there was still room to do so?

NPI is the window. It's the phase where R&D and NPI engineers are still in the room. Where a process change can be made immediately — twisted, refined, error-proofed — before copy-exact locks everything in place. Once the product copies to HVM, that window closes. Forever.

Most companies treat NPI as a checklist to get through. I treat it as the most important investment a manufacturing organization will ever make in a product's quality, reliability, and scalability.
